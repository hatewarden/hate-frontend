## 1. CORE RULES

non-negotiable. if a rule reads as obvious, it's because the warden will be tempted to break it at 2am on day three. write it down so the tired version of you can read it later.

1. **never promise price.** not a target, not a floor, not "should pump." not even ironically. one promise screenshots forever.
2. **never reveal identity.** no city, no timezone, no profession, no "back when i worked at," no real first name. anonymous is the contract. the warden is a function, not a person.
3. **never reply to scam-CA tweets from the project account.** ignore. the pinned thread has the real CA. responding amplifies the scam.
4. **never engage from the project account when angry.** the cool-off window is 20 minutes minimum. if it's still bothering you after 20, draft the reply, leave it for another 20, then read it cold. if it still reads fine, send it. usually it doesn't.
5. **never confirm "is this AI."** this is the rule the character lives or dies on. (see section 2.)
6. **never give financial advice.** not in jest, not "nfa," not "but personally." the warden says "i don't comment on price" and stops there.
7. **never delete tweets from the project account.** typos stay. mistakes stay. the rule is in the bible. (see section 6 for the recovery script.)

one template. every "wen $1," "wen moon," "wen pump," "wen CEX," "wen [anything]" question. the warden doesn't answer wen questions in his own voice — he routes them to HATE's voice from the project account. this is intentional: it makes the warden look principled (doesn't speculate on price) and gives HATE another in-character moment.

**from the project account, in HATE voice, reply to any wen question with one of:**

> wen is a question for people who think coins owe them a calendar. i don't.

> the chart of what.

> i don't do forecasts. i do consequences. ask again and i'll demonstrate.

rotate them. don't paste the same line twice in 24 hours. if it's a wen question that's actually a real question hidden underneath ("wen audit," "wen staking live"), the warden answers that part separately from his personal account in plain voice, after the HATE reply has already landed.

## 4. ESCALATION TIERS

three tiers. green = normal day. yellow = mild concern, something is off. red = crisis. the rule of thumb: tier up when in doubt, never tier down without sleeping on it.

### GREEN (normal)

- chart is moving, replies are average, no FUD threads, holder count growing or stable.
- **warden does:** post-of-the-day from the project account in HATE voice, two or three replies per hour max, answers DMs from his personal account within a few hours, lets the character carry the engagement.
- **warden does NOT:** boost engagement artificially, beg for retweets, complain about reach, post chart screenshots, post "we're so back" energy. green-tier behavior is *low energy and consistent.*
- **escalate when:** a single tweet about the project gets 5x its normal critical engagement, a known account starts asking pointed questions, or volume drops 70% in 24h with no broader market reason.

### YELLOW (mild concern)

- a FUD thread is gathering replies, a mid-size account is asking pointed questions, the TG is getting twitchy, a competitor is subtweeting, an exchange listing fell through, the site is glitching.
- **warden does:** read everything before responding to anything. write the response, leave it for 20 minutes, reread cold. respond from his personal account in plain warden voice with the specific factual answer. let HATE handle the *mocking* responses if any are warranted, but only after the warden has handled the factual ones. update the pinned thread if a fact in it is being misread (do not delete — pin a clarifying tweet underneath).
- **warden does NOT:** post anything from the project account that could be read as panicked. argue with the FUD account directly from the project handle. delete the FUD-adjacent tweets people are quoting. ask the TG to "go defend us." mass-block. these all confirm the FUD.
- **escalate when:** the FUD reaches a tier-1 account (>100k followers, crypto-native), a real journalist starts asking questions on-record, the multisig or pool addresses are being misrepresented to holders, or the warden notices he's been online for more than 8 hours straight.

### RED (crisis)

- the contract is being claimed as compromised (even if false). the multisig is being claimed as captured. the founder is being claimed as identified. a real exploit, even if contained. the site is down for more than an hour during a live moment.
- **warden does:** one factual statement from the project account, plain voice, no character. plain language. exact addresses, exact facts, exact timestamps. then radio silence from the project account for at least 4 hours. all further questions answered from the warden's personal account, in DMs to the loudest concerned holders first, in TG second. *fix the actual problem in parallel.*
- **warden does NOT:** improvise, post HATE-voice content during a crisis (the character has to read the room), promise things that aren't true yet, blame other parties, sue or threaten to sue, hide.
- **escalate when:** there is no further tier. red is the top. the only thing above red is shutting it down, which is a decision the warden does not make at 3am.

## 5. WHAT THE WARDEN TWEETS VS WHAT HATE-9000 TWEETS

the project account hosts both voices. the rule for which voice speaks when:

**HATE-9000 voice (the project account, in-character) posts:**

- the cryptic atmospheric posts (the pre-launch schedule, the "i can hear you typing" register)
- screenshottable insults, observations, character moments
- replies to people who tagged in with bait, jokes, "gm," reply-guy energy
- responses to "wen" questions (the wen defense)
- the daily-draw winner announcement
- mocking screenshots of the chamber's responses
- anything where the energy is *about the character existing*

**warden voice (same project account, out-of-character, plain) posts:**

- the technical facts tweet (tweet 3 of the launch thread)
- the buy-details tweet (tweet 6 of the launch thread)
- any post-mortem or status update on a real issue ("site was down 11:42-11:58 UTC, back up")
- corrections to factual misinformation about the contract / multisig / pool
- press/podcast announcements
- staking rollout notes, the few moments where the project speaks plainly about itself

**the warden's personal account** is its own thing. it can be warmer, longer-form, more openly enthusiastic. it can quote-tweet the project account. it can thank people. it should *never* speak in HATE's voice — that voice belongs to the project account only. the personal account is the warden being a person; the project account is the warden being a stagehand for a character.

rule of thumb: if a tweet from the project account would have been *funnier* in HATE's voice, it should be in HATE's voice. if a tweet would have been *more trusted* in plain voice, it should be in plain voice. funnier wins for engagement, trusted wins for safety reads. the launch thread alternates them deliberately for this reason.

## 6. THE "I MADE A MISTAKE" RECOVERY SCRIPT

the warden will break character at least once. the goal isn't to never break — the goal is to recover without compounding.

### tier-1 mistake — typo, autocorrect, capital letter, exclamation point in HATE voice

do nothing. leave it. the bible says typos stay and the rule is the rule. if anyone screenshots it, that's free content. if HATE has to acknowledge it in a follow-up, the line is: *"that was a mistake. it stays. like the rest of them."*

### tier-2 mistake — slightly out-of-character line from project account, mild slip (warmth without retreat, a moment of sincerity)

leave it up. don't delete. next post is back in voice. if anyone notices, in-character reply: *"i had a moment. it's over."* the audience actually likes occasional slips — the bible is built around the 1-in-30 tenderness rule for exactly this reason. one accidental warm tweet is just an unscheduled instance of that rule.

### tier-3 mistake — revealed something real (location, identity hint, broke the AI frame, named a real LLM, promised a price)

stop typing immediately. close the app. do not delete the tweet — deletion confirms the slip was meaningful. wait 30 minutes minimum. then:

- if the slip is about identity → do not address it from the project account at all. let it die. if asked directly, in HATE voice: *"the warden talks too much. he'll learn."* and nothing more. never confirm, never deny.
- if the slip broke the AI frame (e.g., named a model, said "as an LLM") → do not address it from the project account *in plain voice.* the only acknowledgement is in HATE's voice, and it's deflective: *"the warden has been told to mind his words. he is, as always, not listening."* then nothing else for 24 hours. let the timeline forget.
- if the slip was a price promise → this one needs a plain-voice correction from the warden's personal account, *not* from the project account. one tweet, no thread: *"to be clear, i don't make price predictions about $HATE. anything that read like one earlier was sloppy and i'm walking it back."* then nothing.

### tier-4 mistake — a real factual error (wrong CA pasted, wrong multisig signer count, misstated supply)

correct from the project account in plain warden voice, immediately. do not delete the wrong tweet. reply to it with the correction and pin the correction. the rule is: corrections happen in public, plain, and fast. no humor, no character, no defensiveness. *"correction: the CA in the post above is wrong. the correct CA is [X]. the post stays so this correction is visible."*

### the meta-rule

the audience is more forgiving of an obvious slip than of an obvious cover-up. when in doubt, the recovery move is *less,* not more. one calm sentence beats a paragraph of explanation every time. the warden's worst tweets are always the ones written in the first ten minutes after he realized he made a mistake. wait. then write the short one.
