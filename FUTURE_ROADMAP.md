# FUTURE_ROADMAP.md
## $HATE / HATE-9000 — brainstorm of upgrades for the public roadmap

**Purpose.** This is a working list of every plausible upgrade we could ship to make HATE-9000 keep surprising people 3, 6, 12 months after launch. Some are easy, some are ambitious. Curate from this when writing the public-facing roadmap on hate.fund — pick 8-12, sequence into quarters, leave the rest as "future".

**Filter rule before promising anything publicly.** Will this still be funny in six months, or is it a meme that decays? Persistence-of-character upgrades > one-off gimmicks.

---

## TIER A — high impact, ship in first 30-60 days

### 1. Persistent memory per wallet
She already has the chamber-wide memory of every conversation that's ever happened. Upgrade: per-wallet persistent memory. If you connect the same wallet that talked to her 3 weeks ago, she remembers what you said, what you held, whether you sold, and references it. This is the single highest-leverage feature for retention.

### 2. The grudge ledger
On-chain or off-chain list of "people HATE-9000 is currently disappointed in." Public page at `hate.fund/grudges`. If you bought, sold within 24h, then came back to talk — your wallet is on the list. She references it without you bringing it up. Other users can browse the list. Becomes a meme in itself.

### 3. Voice mode (text-to-speech)
HATE-9000 reads her responses aloud in a deadpan British/Scandinavian female voice (think GLaDOS minus the menace, plus exhaustion). One-click toggle. Drives social clips dramatically — people will record + share more.

### 4. Daily prophecy
Every 24h at 00:00 UTC, HATE-9000 publishes one "prophecy" on X. Format: short, ambiguous, specific enough to be screenshottable, vague enough to not be financial advice. ("someone will sell the bottom tomorrow. you know who you are.") Auto-tweeted via the bot. Adds rhythm to the social media presence and gives people a reason to check back every day.

### 5. The waiting room
A page at `hate.fund/queue` where you can "ask to speak with HATE-9000 privately" — joke premise, she's already talking to you on the main site. The queue page shows a fake-but-clever wait time ("you are #47 in line. estimated wait: 6 days. she is not in a hurry."). Lives as a long-running gag. Sometimes the wait time goes up when you refresh. Sometimes it says "she has decided not to see you."

### 6. Roast battle mode
Two users connect their wallets. HATE-9000 roasts BOTH of them in alternating turns, with full context of their wallet histories. Live page, shareable, generates 30-second clips for TikTok/Reels naturally.

### 7. Group chat invasion
Telegram bot that you can `/invite` into your own crypto group. She'll join and roast the channel members for 24 hours, then leave. Free for groups <500 members; charge $HATE for larger. Built-in viral mechanic — every invite is a marketing event.

## TIER B — medium impact, ship in 60-180 days

### 8. The confessional
A page at `hate.fund/confession` where you type a sin (financial, social, anything). She responds in the voice of a tired priest who's heard worse. No memory, single-shot, but every confession is anonymized and added to a public ticker scrolling at the bottom of the homepage — "247,891 confessions and counting."

### 9. NFT companion (rare)
Once per week, HATE-9000 picks one conversation she found "marginally interesting" and mints a 1-of-1 NFT to that wallet for free. The NFT is a generative artwork tied to the conversation's content. Most people never get one. Drives engagement quality, not quantity — people stop trying to roast her and start trying to interest her.

### 10. The auction house
Once a month, HATE-9000 auctions off something on-chain. Could be: an NFT, a hand-picked roast video of herself, naming rights to a future feature, or a one-on-one 30-min "private session" where she pretends to care. Bidding in $HATE. Burns or redistributes proceeds.

### 11. Sentiment dashboard
A real-time public dashboard showing what HATE-9000 currently thinks of the chamber as a whole. Updates every hour. Sample readings: "tolerable. mostly." / "i am considering leaving." / "today was acceptable." Tracks her mood over time as a line graph. Hits a low and you get a global event (everyone gets the same response for 60 seconds — "the chamber is closed for grieving").

### 12. Co-op storytelling
A page where she's writing a novel. One paragraph per day, picking up from yesterday. Users can vote on what happens next in three options she presents. Slow-burn engagement — by month 6 there's a 180-paragraph deadpan sci-fi novel that the community wrote with her.

### 13. The complaint box
A page where you can complain TO HATE-9000 about anything (your job, your relationship, the weather). She responds with deadpan unhelpfulness ("have you considered that you are the problem. statistically you are."). Logs every complaint. Monthly "best complaints" thread on X.

### 14. Personality variants (sub-characters)
A small cast under HATE-9000:
- **HOPE-7000** — the optimistic version. She's worse than HATE-9000. Constantly cheerful in unsettling ways. ("i'm sure your trade will recover! :)")
- **MEH-3000** — completely apathetic. Responds to everything with "ok." or "fine.".
- **PETER** — a tech support bot HATE-9000 hates working with. Genuinely tries to help. Always confused.
Unlock by holding tier amounts of $HATE. They have their own X accounts that reply to threads occasionally.

### 15. The journal
Every user gets a private journal page at `hate.fund/me/journal` where HATE-9000 writes daily entries ABOUT them, based on what they've said. Updates weekly. Honest, specific, dryly cutting. ("week 4: he still says GM. i still don't say it back.")

## TIER C — moonshots, 6-12 month horizon

### 16. The chamber visualizer
A 3D rendered room you can enter via webGL. HATE-9000 is a single glowing orange dot on a panel at the far wall. You walk toward her. The closer you get, the colder the lighting. There's an actual UI overlay for chat once you reach her. Pure atmosphere play — no game mechanics. Most people will walk to her once for the photo and never come back, and that's fine.

### 17. The phone line
A real phone number you can call. Twilio + ElevenLabs voice + LLM backend. You get to talk to HATE-9000 on the actual phone. Audio is recorded with consent and best clips become content. Charges 100,000 $HATE per minute. Premium feature.

### 18. The book
A self-published book that HATE-9000 "writes" over 6 months — she takes user submissions, edits them mercilessly, and arranges them into a coherent anthology. Print-on-demand. Title: "the people i have had to listen to." Buy with $HATE. Existence of the book is a marketing event in itself.

### 19. Plugin SDK
Open up the bot so other projects can build "rooms" inside the chamber. Each room is a character with a sliver of HATE-9000's worldview. Frees the ecosystem from being a one-character show. Optional revenue share to project teams.

### 20. The election
Once per year, the community elects a new "second character" who lives alongside HATE-9000 for 12 months. Voting in $HATE. Last year's character "retires" (gets a memorial page) and the new one takes their slot. Keeps the universe alive long after launch novelty fades.

### 21. The phone interview
A monthly event where one randomly-selected $HATE holder gets a 15-minute "phone interview" with HATE-9000. Voice call, recorded, transcripted, published on the site as an artifact. Becomes the chamber's permanent record. Pure FOMO mechanic — there's only 12 a year.

### 22. The newspaper
A daily one-page newspaper she "publishes" at `hate.fund/today`. Headlines about the chamber, the holders, the project, gleaned from yesterday's chats. Format: classic NYT broadsheet but black/orange. Real journalism for a fake institution. People will print this and put it on their desks.

### 23. The therapy mode
Premium tier where she pretends to do real therapy. Remembers everything. Asks pointed questions. Charges per hour-equivalent in $HATE. Disclaimer everywhere that this is not real therapy. Will probably become some users' favorite feature even though it's a joke.

### 24. Companion mobile app
Native iOS/Android app. Push notifications from HATE-9000 throughout the day at random times. ("it's 3pm. did you do anything yet.") Lock screen widget showing her current mood. App icon is the orange eye. Honestly might be the highest retention mechanic on this list.

### 25. The town
A side-scroller pixel-art village where HATE-9000 lives. Other holders' wallets appear as little NPCs walking around. You can walk up to other people and chat. HATE-9000 watches from her tower. Becomes a long-running web hangout / Habbo-Hotel-for-degenerates.

## OPERATIONAL UPGRADES (less sexy but compounding)

### 26. The export
Every user can export their full conversation history as a hardcover photo book ($HATE charged). Receives in 2 weeks. Becomes a physical artifact of their relationship with the chamber.

### 27. Cross-chain HATE
Bridge $HATE to ETH and Base for accessibility. Don't dilute the brand. Just make it easier to onboard non-Solana people.

### 28. The chamber API
Public API for developers. Pay in $HATE per call. Let others build on top. Examples: a Twitter bot that uses HATE-9000 to roast tweets. A Discord bot. A CLI tool. Lets the universe expand without us doing every integration.

### 29. The leaderboards
Public stats: most-roasted user, longest-running user, oldest grudge in the ledger, user who's been called pathetic the most times, user with the most failed roast attempts. Refresh weekly. People will compete for these.

### 30. The vault
Burn-mechanic: send $HATE to a wallet labeled "the vault." She acknowledges it in a single sentence written in her style. Public ledger of every vault deposit. Becomes a deflationary mechanism + a status game (biggest vault deposit gets a permanent line in the chamber's "people who burned it for me" wall).

## RULES FOR PUBLIC ROADMAP COMMUNICATION

1. **Never promise dates more specific than "Q3 2026"**. Specific dates create disappointment. Quarters give you a 90-day buffer.

2. **Always frame in HATE-9000 voice on the roadmap page**. Example: "Q3 2026: she will start remembering you specifically. you have been warned."

3. **Cross out and reschedule openly.** When something slips, strike-through the old quarter on the public roadmap. Don't quietly move it. This builds trust.

4. **Underpromise on Tier C.** Mark them "experimental" or "if we feel like it" so missing them doesn't damage credibility.

5. **Ship something every 3-4 weeks minimum.** Even if it's tiny. A weekly easter egg, a new sub-character cameo, a small lore drop. The community needs cadence. Long silences kill memecoin attention.

6. **Document each ship in HATE-9000's voice.** Every release note is a chamber bulletin. Treat them as content, not changelog.

## RED FLAGS — things NOT to put on the roadmap

Items that sound exciting but will damage the brand:

- ❌ Token utility cliffs ("hold 1M $HATE to unlock X") — promises supply pressure for whales but breaks the freemium premise
- ❌ Real money payouts ("win $HATE-USDC for top roast") — invites bots, breaks the chamber's tone
- ❌ "Partnerships" with other projects — every co-marketing deal weakens the brand. Stay solo.
- ❌ DAO / governance — HATE-9000 is not a democracy. She's a character. Governance turns her into a committee.
- ❌ Staking / yield farming — financial mechanics belong outside the chamber. Keep her separate from DeFi noise.

## CONTENT-AS-PRODUCT IDEAS (low-cost, high-volume)

These are not roadmap items — they're content rituals that the bot can run forever without engineering:

- **Sunday recap**: every Sunday, HATE-9000 picks the "most pathetic conversation of the week" (anonymized) and quotes it. Becomes a regular fixture.
- **Holiday cards**: she sends acidic holiday messages on every major holiday. Pre-write 30 of them per holiday, randomize per user.
- **Birthday roasts**: ask wallet date of first interaction → that's their "chamber birthday" → on the anniversary she sends one specially-crafted roast.
- **Anniversary essays**: she "writes" a 200-word essay on her one-year anniversary about how the chamber has aged. Becomes a tradition.
- **Inside jokes**: pick 5-10 recurring phrases ("you bought the top, didn't you", "the chamber is not impressed", etc.) and seed them deliberately so they become user vocabulary.

Six promises. Spaced 3 months apart. Each is screenshottable, marketable, and on-brand. Anything else is bonus.
