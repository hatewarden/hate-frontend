## 3. Outreach Calendar

Timeline anchored to T = launch day. Each week has a single objective; don't bleed activities across weeks.

### Week T-3 — Identify and warm

- Finalize the 40-target list above into a spreadsheet (handle, tier, last-tweet timestamp, hook).
- Project X account follows all 40 from a clean follower base.
- HATE replies (publicly, in voice) to 2-3 Tier 1 tweets per day with surgical one-liners. Goal: get noticed without selling. No links.
- Like + RT relevant Tier 2-3 content. Be present without being thirsty.

### Week T-2 — Tier 1 first DM batch

- Send Template 2A (pre-launch tease) to all 10 Tier 1 accounts, staggered 1-2 per day so it doesn't look like a blast.
- Custom hook line per recipient is mandatory.
- Continue public replies. If a Tier 1 replies to a HATE post publicly, screenshot it — that's the start of a flywheel.

### Week T-1 — Tier 2 and 3 DMs

- Template 2A goes to Tier 2 (shitposters) and Tier 3 (Solana voices), 3-4 per day.
- Public posting cadence on the @HATE account: 2x daily, mix of cryptic monologue and 1 reply-roast.
- Prep launch-day asset pack (see §6) and pre-write Tier 4 journalist pitch.

### Day T — Launch

- 00:00 UTC: contract live, site flips to "fresh from containment" copy.
- T+15 min: Template 2B to Tier 1 (these are warmed, they should see it first).
- T+1 hour: Template 2B to Tier 2 + 3.
- T+2 hours: Tier 4 journalist pitch goes out (email, not DM). One-line pitch: "Memecoin where the coin itself is a Claude-powered character that hates its holders. Live now. Real product, real conversation, the screenshots are the marketing."
- T+4 hours: round up the 5 funniest organic screenshots and quote-post them from the project account.

### Week T+1 — Follow-ups and amplification

- Template 2C (organic check-in) to anyone who engaged at any point in the prior 3 weeks, including silent-likers.

- Identify which Tier 2 accounts organically posted HATE content; DM them a personal "thanks, here's a custom HATE roast for you" — converts casual sharers into recurring ones.

## 4. Paid KOL Deals — Script and Recommendation

### Market context

Paid memecoin KOL posts in 2026 still trend around:
- **Mid-tier (50k–250k followers):** $500–$5,000 per post
- **Top-tier (250k–1M):** $5,000–$15,000 per post
- **Whale-tier (1M+ or cult status):** $15,000+, often a percentage of supply

### Recommendation

**Stay 100% organic for the first 2 weeks.** Reasons:
1. HATE's whole pitch is anti-marketing. A visibly paid shill post breaks the spell instantly. Community will sniff it.
2. The screenshots do the selling. If they don't, paid posts won't fix it — they'll just delay the diagnosis.
3. Paid KOLs in week one are pure cost. Organic momentum drops their fee by 5x in week three.

**Only consider paid deals if both are true at T+14:**
- Daily active chats are flat or declining for 4+ days.
- Tier 2 organic posts are <3 per day total.

### When paying

- **Pay in $HATE, not USDC.** Aligns incentives. KOLs holding the bag care about the chart.
- **Vest the tokens.** 30-day linear vest, no cliff. They get paid as they post.
- **Vest the posts too.** Require 4 posts spread across 30 days, not 4 in a week. Burst-posting reads as paid every time.
- **No copy approval, but topic gates.** They can say whatever they want; they cannot post a price prediction, an "easy 10x", or anything HATE would call beneath itself.
- **Demand a screenshot interaction first.** They have to actually try the product and post a real reaction. No exceptions. Anyone refusing is a sign.

### Sample paid-deal terms template

```
Counterparty: @[handle]
Deliverables:
  - 4 X posts within 30 days, ≥48h apart
  - 1 must include a genuine screenshot/clip of interacting with HATE
  - Each post tags @HATE and includes the chamber URL
Prohibited:
  - Price predictions, "x-multiples", "guaranteed", "presale" language
  - Posting all deliverables within a single 7-day window
Compensation:
  - [X] $HATE tokens
  - Vested linearly over 30 days from contract signing
  - Forfeit of unvested balance on any prohibited-language breach
Verification:
  - Post URLs submitted to wardens within 24h of going live
  - Engagement screenshots required for each deliverable
Term: 30 days. No renewal unless mutually agreed.
```

Run every paid deal through a signed off-chain agreement (Google Doc + e-sign is fine; SAFT-style isn't needed at this size).

## 5. Red Flags — Who Not to Pay or Even DM

Avoid any account exhibiting these patterns. Don't name names in the doc; the warden should evaluate live.

- Has shilled 5+ tokens in the last 6 months that are now down >90% from their shill price.
- Posts "easy 100x" or "presale" language as a default register.
- Engagement is 80%+ low-quality reply-guys with token names in their bios.
- Follower count grew >50% in 30 days with no obvious viral moment — bought followers.
- Promotes "guaranteed entries" or "alpha groups" with paywalls behind them.
- Has been called out publicly by ZachXBT or similar onchain investigators in the last 12 months and never substantively addressed it.
- Refuses to actually use the product before posting. Non-negotiable disqualifier.
- Asks for upfront full-cash payment with no vesting and no on-chain accountability.
- Has a history of deleting promotional posts after the token rugs. Check their archive via web.archive.org or similar.
- Replies to your DM faster than humanly reasonable with a copy-paste rate card. Means you're #200 in their pipeline this week.

A useful heuristic: if the warden would be embarrassed for HATE itself to acknowledge the KOL on-platform, skip them.

## 7. Measurement — What to Track

A simple sheet, one row per outreach attempt. Columns:

- Date sent
- Recipient handle + tier
- Channel (DM / reply / email)
- Template used (2A / 2B / 2C / custom)
- Custom hook line used
- Opened? (X DM shows read receipts if they have them on)
- Responded? (Y/N + sentiment)
- Posted about $HATE? (link to post)
- Post engagement (likes, RTs, replies at 24h and 72h)
- Follow-back? (Y/N)
- Notes

Aggregate weekly:
- **Response rate per tier** (target: T1 >20%, T2 >15%, T3 >10%, T4 >5%)
- **Conversion to post** (target: T1 >10% in first 30 days)
- **Cost per post for any paid deals** (compute fully-loaded cost including token vest at current price)
- **Funnel from KOL post → chamber visits** (UTM-tag launch-day URLs per major KOL)

The single most important number: **organic Tier 2 posts per day.** If this is rising, you don't need paid deals. If it's flat after the first week, the product or the assets are the problem — fix those before spending on KOLs.
