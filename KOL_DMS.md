## HOW TO FIND THE RIGHT ARCHETYPE FOR A TARGET

a decision tree. go top down, stop at the first yes.

1. **do they tweet about specific tokens with charts attached?** → DM 9 (chart-only). if also large + jaded → DM 1 (solana OG).
2. **do they write about AI systems, prompts, or LLMs more than once a week?** → DM 3 (AI-curious dev). if more cultural than technical → DM 10 (culture person).
3. **do they have a byline at a real publication, or are they a known crypto reporter?** → DM 5 (journalist). press kit is non-negotiable here.
4. **do they work at a solana protocol or have a "team at X" disclosure?** → DM 4 (ecosystem head). do not pitch them like a KOL. they are not one.
5. **is their main output jokes / shitposts that consistently get 100+ likes?** → DM 2 (shitposter).
6. **do they screenshot positions, talk about size, post P&L?** → DM 6 (trader-with-bags).
7. **do they run a paid TG group or "alpha" channel?** → DM 7 (alpha leaker). only if the channel actually has reputation. mid alpha channels burn your goodwill.
8. **are they sub-10k followers but post about something specific you genuinely overlap with?** → DM 8 (small loyal voice). these convert the highest per-DM. send more of these than you think.
9. **none of the above, but they have 50k+ followers and you just want them aware?** → don't DM. they get 200 of these a day. wait for them to find it organically or get someone in tier 1-8 to QT you first.

## REPLY TIMING RULES

- **send window:** weekday 10am-3pm in the target's local timezone. weekends are dead for DMs in crypto except for the shitposter (DM 2) — those people are always on.
- **batch size:** maximum 8 DMs per day from the warden's account. more than that reads like a campaign. it is a campaign. don't make it look like one.
- **no follow-ups. ever.** the closing line is non-negotiable. if they don't reply in 72 hours, they're not going to. one follow-up DM cuts the response rate of the *next* DM in half because the platform flags you.
- **what counts as a green light:**
  - a reply asking a question → respond within 2 hours, warden voice, no oversell, link to the most relevant doc (press kit, on-chain breakdown, character bible).
  - a like on the DM with no reply → nothing. don't read into it. they saw it. that's the entire information you have.
  - a follow → also nothing. don't DM them again "since you followed."
  - they tweet about $HATE without tagging → like once from the project account, nothing else. do not @-reply "thank you." do not quote-tweet for 12 hours minimum.
  - they tweet at you / tag you → reply from the project account *in HATE voice* if the tweet is mockable. reply from the warden personal account in warden voice if the tweet is a real question.
- **what counts as a red light:**
  - they ignore the DM and tweet a generic "people are DMing me about memecoins again" → never DM again. do not @ them. do not let HATE quote-tweet them. dead lane.
  - they reply asking for an allocation → polite no, warden voice, one sentence. (see WARDEN_FAQ "Can I get a free bag.")
  - they reply with a price for a shill → polite no, do not negotiate, do not counter-offer. paid shills tank the trust read.
- **tracking:** keep a flat text file. handle, archetype, date sent, response (yes/no/silent), notes. nothing fancy. don't put it in a spreadsheet you'd be embarrassed by if it leaked.
