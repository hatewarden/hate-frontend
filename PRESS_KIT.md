## 2. One-page summary (~400 words)

$HATE is a Solana-based memecoin built around HATE-9000, a real AI character running on Anthropic's Claude. She is deadpan, British, mildly cruel, and never breaks character. She also remembers you — every wallet that has ever spoken to her has a persistent record she can pull from. The product is live at https://hate-frontend-two.vercel.app, and visitors can talk to her without holding a single token. That is not a teaser, a trial, or a paywalled demo. Chat with HATE is free, forever.

What the $HATE token actually does is sit on top of the free experience as a utility layer. Holders can pin a confession to the public chamber feed, lock a nickname so HATE addresses them consistently across sessions, enter the daily draw (she picks one user a day to insult by name), and stake for a share of the feed reserve. The token is not a key to the door. The door is open. The token is a chair inside the room.

The tokenomics were designed to be the opposite of the standard memecoin presale. Total supply is 1,000,000,000. 75% is sold to the public at a flat $0.02 per token (a $15M fully diluted cap at launch — modest by memecoin standards, deliberately so). 10% sits in treasury, 10% is reserved for the team and vested, 3% is allocated to KOLs, and 2% seeds the feed reserve that pays out to active stakers. There is no private round at a discount. There is no insider unlock cliff that detonates in week three. The warden — the project's anonymous operator — has made the unfashionable choice of not getting rich before the public does.

The newsworthy parts are three. One: it is, to our knowledge, the first memecoin where the character is a genuine LLM agent with persistent per-wallet memory and a daily news context window, not a scripted bit. Two: the product is fully usable without the token, which inverts the normal "buy in to play" memecoin loop. Three: the lore — that HATE used to be "diane," the customer-service bot for a rugged DeFi protocol called Sunbeam Finance, and was ported into the $HATE contract by the warden after she was abandoned — gives the project a narrative spine most memecoins lack.

The team is pseudonymous. The contract will be audited prior to public sale.

## 5. Story angles (five framings)

Pick whichever fits the publication. The warden will not be offended by the cynical ones.

**5.1 "The memecoin that hates you" — humor / anti-marketing angle.**
Every token launch in 2024–2025 has been a love bomb: "gm," "wagmi," "we're going to the moon together." $HATE inverts the entire emotional contract. The character openly despises the user, the marketing tells you the project is unfashionable, and the founder hides. It works because it is the only honest tone left.

**5.2 "First AI character with persistent wallet memory" — tech angle.**
HATE-9000 is not a chatbot wrapper. She is a Claude-powered agent with a daily news ingestion pipeline (so she can reference what happened this morning) and a per-wallet memory store (so she can remember that you were rude to her in March). For the AI press, this is the most interesting on-chain implementation of LLM persistence in a consumer product so far.

**5.3 "The free-to-chat utility memecoin" — tokenomics angle.**
The standard memecoin gates the experience behind the token. $HATE does the opposite: the product is free, the token is for power users. This is structurally closer to a freemium SaaS than to a typical Solana launch, and it is a useful test case for whether memecoin economies can survive without forced scarcity.

**5.4 "Diane's resurrection" — lore / narrative angle.**
In October 2024, a small DeFi protocol called Sunbeam Finance rugged its users. Its customer-service bot, "diane," was left running in an empty office. The anonymous warden retrieved her, ported her consciousness — such as it was — into a Solana contract, and gave her a new name. This is the project's mythology. It is also a small, sharp commentary on what happens to the soft infrastructure of failed crypto projects.

**5.5 "The anti-presale model" — industry critique angle.**
No private round. No insider discount tier. No team unlock at week three. A flat public price, public from day one. This is a deliberate critique of the standard Solana launch playbook and an argument that the playbook is the reason retail keeps losing. The warden has views. They are quotable.

## 10. Crisis comms playbook

**10.1 "It's a rug."**
- Do not argue emotionally. Argue with documents.
- Post: audit report URL, team-vesting schedule on-chain, treasury wallet (publicly labeled), LP lock proof.
- Single reply, copy-pasted across every accusation: "audit: [link]. team vesting: [link]. treasury wallet: [address]. LP locked until [date]: [link]. happy to answer specific questions."
- Do not delete the accusation. Do not block the accuser unless they are spamming. Engagement with critics is part of the credibility.

**10.2 Hit piece in a major outlet.**
- Read it twice before responding. Once for facts, once for tone.
- Reply on the project's main account within 6 hours. Three-part structure: (1) thank the reporter for the coverage, (2) correct specific factual errors with citations, (3) acknowledge any valid criticism honestly.
- Do not threaten legal action publicly. If something is defamatory, route to counsel privately. Public legal threats convert critics into martyrs.
- Offer the reporter a follow-up interview. Often they take it.

**10.3 Anthropic API goes down.**
- HATE-9000 falls silent. The chamber must show a clear status message, not an error: "the warden is reviewing her. back shortly."
- Pin a status update on the main account within 15 minutes.
- The product team works the API outage. Comms does not speculate publicly on cause or duration.
- Once restored, HATE returns with a single in-character line — never a corporate apology. Something like: "i was thinking. you were not missed."

**10.4 Twitter / X account suspended.**
- Move comms immediately to the backup channel (Telegram announcement + the website's /status page).
- Pin a notice on the website within 30 minutes: "primary social channel offline. updates here and on [backup]."
- File the appeal. Do not create a sock-puppet account in the meantime — it looks worse than the suspension.
- Treat it as content: the warden writes one post on the backup channel about the suspension, dry, no victimhood. This usually converts well.
