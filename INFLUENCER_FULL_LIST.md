## INBOUND vs OUTBOUND — what to expect

Once your launch thread is live, you'll get **inbound DMs** from creators wanting to promote you. Be MORE skeptical of inbound:

- Many are bot-farm accounts dressed up as "crypto creators"
- Many are agencies aggregating 10 low-quality TikTokers to sell as a "package"
- The legitimate inbound DMs are usually from creators who:
  - Have a track record (you can find their work)
  - Don't lead with payment terms (they lead with interest in the project)
  - Have a SPECIFIC angle they want to take ("I want to do a piece about the chamber concept")

If an inbound looks legit, respond with the Template 2 from INFLUENCER_DEAL_TEMPLATES.md. If it looks farmed, ignore — don't even reply.

## REGIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

Different regions have different creator scenes:

- **English / US**: covered above, primary market
- **Spanish-speaking**: surprisingly large crypto creator scene in LATAM. Search `cripto memecoin solana` on TikTok/YouTube. Often cheaper than English-language creators with comparable reach.
- **Brazilian Portuguese**: similar — `solana memecoin` PT-BR has a real audience
- **Turkish**: large crypto retail audience, smaller creator scene but cheap placements
- **Korean / Japanese / Chinese**: separate ecosystems with their own platforms (Naver, Niconico, Weibo). Don't try to enter these as an English-only project — placements without proper localization burn money

If your week 2-3 budget allows: 1 Spanish-language placement + 1 Portuguese-language placement is high-ROI vs cramming more English creators. Different audience, less crowded.
