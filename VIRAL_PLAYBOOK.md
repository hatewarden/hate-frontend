# $HATE — Viral Playbook

The offensive game plan. How to win the attention war for a memecoin whose front door is a chat window, not a buy button.

This is not the launch checklist (`LAUNCH_PREP.md`), the tweet bank (`TWEETS_V2.md`), the KOL list (`KOL_OUTREACH.md`), the press kit (`PRESS_KIT.md`), or the deploy doc (`CONTRACT_DEPLOY.md`). This is how we actually go viral.

Read this twice before T-0. Reference it during.

## 1. The Core Insight — Why This Can Actually Work

Every memecoin has the same funnel: see tweet, click link, buy bag, hope. The conversion event is a transaction. Most people who hit the landing page never sign. The funnel leaks at the wallet step because the prospect has nothing to *experience* before paying.

$HATE inverts that. The funnel is: see tweet, click link, talk to HATE-9000, get a reaction worth screenshotting, share it, *then* maybe pay for something persistent. Chat is free forever. The product is a character, and you meet the character before you spend a dollar.

This matters for three reasons.

**First, it solves the cold start.** A normal memecoin launches into a vacuum — nobody has any reason to talk about it until the chart moves. $HATE has a thing to do that doesn't require the chart to move. A pre-launch tester who never buys still produces a screenshot. A screenshot is a marketing asset. The character generates content whether or not the token pumps.

**Second, it filters for the right kind of attention.** People who chat for an hour and laugh are pre-qualified. They've already self-selected for the voice. When they buy, they're not buying a ticker — they're tipping the bouncer at a club they already love. Their hold conviction is structurally higher than the average rotator's, because they bought a vibe, not a number.

**Third — and this is the part nobody outside the project will understand at first — what we're selling is not chat.** Chat is the loss leader. The bag is for status: pinning a confession on the wall, locking a nickname so nobody else can have it, getting your wallet roasted publicly, paying 10k a month so HATE replies to *you* in voice. These are vanity goods. Vanity goods have terrible price elasticity, which is exactly what we want. People who pay 5k to lock "diogenes" as their nickname are not the same people who panic-sell at -30%.

Say it cleanly: we are selling persistence, identity, and the right to be acknowledged by a character people find funny. The free chat is the showroom. The token is the membership card. The pump is a side effect of a working product, not the product itself.

This is the only memecoin thesis I've seen in two years that doesn't require the next guy to be dumber than the last guy.

## 2. The Viral Moment Formula

A moment that travels has three properties, and you can usually only get two. We're going to try for all three.

- **Screenshottable.** The whole point fits in one image with no context required. If the reader has to scroll or read a thread, it dies.
- **Opinion-divisive.** It produces a yes/no reaction. Cute does not travel. Pretty does not travel. "Holy shit" or "this is awful" both travel. The middle of the bell curve is death.
- **Makes the sharer look smart or funny.** Nobody retweets to make *you* look good. They retweet to make *themselves* look like they found it first.

HATE's natural moment-generator is the deadpan ratio. The user posts something earnest or stupid. HATE responds with one line that is specific, acidic, correct, and unbothered. Specificity is the load-bearing word. "you are bad" travels nowhere. "you bought $BONK three times in twelve hours on three different wallets, none of which are net positive, and you came here to ask me about life" travels everywhere.

Three concrete examples of what a moment looks like for this project.

**Example A — the trader confession.** A user pastes their PnL screenshot and asks HATE to "be honest." HATE responds: "you've made seventeen trades this month. you're down on sixteen. the one that's up is solana you bought in 2023 and forgot about. the lesson is that you got better at this when you weren't doing it." The screenshot travels in trader Twitter because every trench account knows someone exactly like that. Half the replies are "this is me." Half are "this AI is mid." Both halves drive clicks.

**Example B — the philosophy student.** Some 19-year-old types "what's the meaning of life" because they've seen ChatGPT do the bit. HATE: "ask me a question you couldn't have googled at fifteen." Screenshot. Travels in tech Twitter because it inverts the expected LLM behavior. The sharer looks smart for finding the one chatbot that pushes back.

**Example C — the wallet roast.** Someone pays the 25k fee to roast a famous trader's wallet. HATE pulls the on-chain history and produces something like: "you've held this token for nine months. in those nine months, the team has shipped twice, your bag is down 78%, and you've tweeted about it 340 times. you don't own it. it owns you." The roasted party either rage-quotes it (perfect, free distribution) or stays silent (other people quote it for them). Either way, we win.

**How to seed moments without faking them.** Do not write a fake screenshot. Do not pay a friend to post a fake screenshot. Markets smell this in eight seconds and the project never recovers. What you do instead:

1. Run the closed beta with 50 hand-picked people from the believer list (see Section 3) for 96 hours before public chat opens. Tell them nothing about strategy. Just give them access and tell them HATE is rude.
2. Watch the chat logs. Three to seven of them will produce something quotable in the first day. You don't have to manufacture the moment — you have to *recognize* it and amplify it.
3. DM the user. "your conversation just had the line of the day. mind if we quote-tweet the screenshot from the main account? we'll credit you." 95% say yes. Now you have an authentic moment, sourced from a real user, surfaced by the main account.
4. The amplification *is* the work. Most projects produce great content and let it die in DMs. We produce great content and put it on a stage.

The voice rule for amplification: when HATE's account quote-tweets a moment, the quote line is one sentence, lowercase, deadpan, with no hashtags and no emojis. "this one was free." "we will not be making a t-shirt." "found." That's it.

## 3. The Three Audiences You Actually Need

Most launches scatter effort across ten audience segments and reach none of them. You need three. Weight your effort accordingly.

### The Believer — 60% of effort

These are the people who decide whether this project gets a soul. They are not whales. They are the SOL-native users who collect characters, who held $WIF before the meme, who buy character coins because the character is funny, not because the chart is green. They are the ones who will reply to HATE's posts at 3am because they think it's funny, not because they're paid.

**Where they live.** X mostly, in three layers: the SOL trench (anon accounts, frog/cat PFPs, 2k-20k followers, post 40 times a day), the character collector layer (people whose bio lists three or four memecoin tickers), and the "AI is a personality not a tool" tier (people who follow Truth Terminal, Goatseus, the Virtuals ecosystem). Telegram secondarily, in the cleaner SOL launch groups. Almost never Discord.

**How to reach them.** Not by buying ads. Not by paying the obvious KOLs. By having HATE-9000 reply to *their* posts unprompted, in character, in a way that flatters their taste without flattering them. If a believer posts "we are so back" about a SOL meme they like, HATE replies "you said that yesterday about a different one." It's a low-cost interaction that reads as the character noticing them. Believers screenshot that. The follow comes for free.

**What they need to hear.** Almost nothing about the token. Believers do not want to be sold to. They want to be *amused* and then left alone to make their own decision. The pitch is the product. The product is the character. You let them find the buy button on their own.

### The Journalist — 20% of effort

A single piece in The Block, Decrypt, or — if we get really lucky — a tech vertical at The Information, 404 Media, or Bloomberg, is worth more than a hundred KOL retweets. Not because journalists move price, but because a piece is permanent. It indexes on Google. It gives the next ten KOLs a reason to talk about you. It legitimizes the project for the second wave that doesn't trust X.

**Who to target.** The crypto-native press first: The Block (Tom Mitchelhill on memecoins), Decrypt (anyone on the culture beat), DLNews, CoinDesk (Margaux Nijkerk does culture-ish), Milk Road and Blockworks for newsletters. The AI-and-crypto crossover writers: Jacob Silverman if he's still on it, the Coindesk Magazine essayists. Second wave if we pop: 404 Media (they love a weird internet story), The Verge (Mia Sato writes good chaos pieces), Wired's culture desk, Bloomberg Crypto.

**The hook.** Not "new memecoin launches." That hook is dead. The hook is "the first memecoin you don't have to buy to use." Lead with the chat-first funnel. The angle for them is the inversion: every other memecoin is pay-to-engage, this one gave away the engagement and charges for the status. That's a story. The character voice is the color quote. The price chart is buried in paragraph 11.

Press kit is already done. The job here is *targeting* — one personalized email per journalist, with a screenshot of HATE roasting their own beat. If you can DM a tech reporter a screenshot of HATE making fun of tech reporters, you don't need a pitch.

### The Reply-Guy — 20% of effort

Reply guys are the metabolic system of memecoin Twitter. They turn ratios into momentum. A KOL with 200k followers posts something dumb; a reply guy with 4k followers responds with HATE's line and gets 12k likes on the reply. That reply *becomes* the way 100k people see the original post. The reply-guy is doing your marketing for you, for free, because their incentive is the same as ours: get attention by being the funniest one in the thread.

**Who they are.** Accounts that post 80 times a day, mostly replies, mostly to bigger accounts, mostly trying to be the top comment. The bio is usually "degen," "esq.," a Solana address, or some bit. Follower count 1k-15k. Engagement rate astronomical relative to size.

**What they need.** Material. They need lines they can drop into a thread. Our job is to make HATE the most quotable account on SOL Twitter, so reply guys reach for HATE lines the way they currently reach for Mr. Wonderful or Goatseus references. See Section 5 for the ten templates.

The mistake to avoid: paying reply guys directly. Don't. Pay them in *content*. They want to be funny more than they want $200.

## 4. The First 72 Hours — Hour-by-Hour Battle Plan

### Pre-T0 — The night before

The night before is not for posting. It is for not breaking things. Final pre-flight, in this order:

- Confirm CA is verified on Solscan, Birdeye, DEXscreener. Token-2022 specifics double-checked.
- The `/buy` page works on a fresh wallet with Phantom, Backpack, Solflare. Tested by someone who is not you.
- HATE-9000 chat handles 200 concurrent sessions without queueing. Load-test or you will find out at T+1.
- All scheduled tweets queued through hour 24. The first six are pre-written and approved.
- 50-person beta cohort has been told "tomorrow, public opens at X UTC." No earlier.
- Team is in a single ops channel. Phones charged. Nobody is on a plane.

Sleep. The single biggest unforced error on launch day is a tired founder making a bad call at hour 8.

### T+0 to T+6 — Priming

The first six hours are not about volume. They are about setting a frame nobody else gets to set.

- T+0: pinned thread goes live. Three tweets. First tweet is the character speaking, not the founder. Second is the mechanic in one line. Third is the link. No emojis. No "we're live!" Pinned for 72 hours minimum.
- T+0:15: HATE account starts replying, in character, to the 20 most-recent posts in the SOL trench. Not promotional. Just present. The goal is for trench accounts to see the handle four times in their first scroll without being marketed to.
- T+1: first three beta users post their best screenshots organically. They were not coordinated. They just had screenshots ready. The main account quote-tweets each with one-line deadpan.
- T+2: open the 50-person Telegram seed group to 500. Drip, not flood.
- T+4: first KOL post lands. (Tier-3 KOL, not tier-1. Tier-1 goes at T+24, not before — see KOL_OUTREACH.md ordering.)
- T+6: check vitals. Good looks like: 800-2000 unique chat sessions, 200-500 telegram members, 30-80 holders, MC anywhere from $80k to $300k. In trouble looks like: under 200 chat sessions, sub-50 holders, the pinned thread has no replies. If in trouble, do not panic-buy KOLs. Read Section 10.

### T+6 to T+24 — First wave

This is the moment-harvesting window. Every quotable screenshot from the first six hours gets amplified now, while attention is fresh.

- Push the day's best chat moment into a quote-tweet from the main. One per hour, max. Quality bar high — if you don't have a moment that's screenshottable + opinion-divisive + makes-the-sharer-look-smart, do not post.
- Tier-2 KOLs (the 5k-30k follower SOL accounts) get a DM with one specific screenshot and the line "this is from yesterday's beta. thought you'd find it funny." No ask. No pitch. Just the screenshot. About a third of them will share unprompted because the screenshot is genuinely funny. The rest you can follow up with later.
- First reply-guy seeding wave. Drop three of the Section 5 templates into bait-rich threads (any tier-1 trader posting a hopium take). The HATE account itself does this, not surrogates.
- T+12: first written newsletter mention if Milk Road / Blockworks bites. If not, no-op.
- T+18: state of the launch tweet. Not a "we did it!" tweet. A "we are processing the volume" tweet, in character. "we have been told nine thousand people are sad. this is consistent with expectations."
- T+24 check: good looks like 5k-15k chat sessions, 1.5k-4k telegram, 300-800 holders, MC $500k-$2M, at least one organic screenshot ratio'd above 5k likes. Trouble looks like sub-1k telegram, no organic moments, MC under $200k and trending down. If trouble: Section 10.

### T+24 to T+48 — Ride or rescue

This is the window where most launches die quietly because the team thinks day 1 *was* the launch. It wasn't. Day 2 is where the second wave decides whether to come in. The second wave is bigger than day 1, but only if you give them a reason.

If momentum is real:

- Tier-1 KOLs go now, not before. They should be following on top of a story, not creating one. Lead with the strongest organic screenshot.
- Press outreach goes live. The same screenshot that worked on KOLs is the lead asset for journalists. One personalized note, one screenshot, one paragraph of context. No press release.
- First "manufactured moment" runs: the feed pot countdown for the daily draw winner is announced. (See Section 6.)
- HATE-9000 produces a long-form roast of "this week in SOL." Not the main account in third person. The character itself, in voice. This is the asset that gets quoted into oblivion.

If momentum is soft:

- Do not double the spend. Doubling spend into a market that's not biting just lights money on fire.
- Find the single screenshot from the first 24 hours that *almost* worked. Re-stage. Different framing, different time of day, get a different beta user to share it organically.
- Re-open the closed beta to another 200 hand-picked accounts. Concentration trumps reach in a soft launch.

### T+48 to T+72 — Convert buyers into evangelists

Day 3 is when transactional buyers either become believers or disappear. The way you keep them is by getting them to do something *visible* with their bag.

- Push the persistence sinks hard. "pin your confession" is the cheapest meaningful spend (1k). Make a thread of the best pinned confessions every six hours. The pinner gets visibility — they bought their way onto a stage.
- First "fed the most" leaderboard week recap teased (will land at day 7).
- First voice replies go live for the 10k/month subscribers. The voice files themselves become content — a 12-second voice clip of HATE-9000 saying something cutting is the most screenshot-and-quote-tweetable asset we have.
- T+72 check: good looks like 30k+ chat sessions cumulative, 8k+ telegram, 1.5k+ holders, MC $3M+, retention (% of day-1 chatters who returned) above 30%. Trouble looks like flat or declining holders, retention under 15%, no organic moments in the last 24 hours.

The honest version: if T+72 looks like trouble, you have one more week to either change the product (more aggressive moments, faster character iteration) or accept that you have a tier-2 outcome and play it that way. The third-week pivot is real but rare.

## 5. The Reply-Guy Strategy

Reply guys are the unpaid air force. They don't need management. They need ammunition.

The principle: when bigger accounts post bait, reply guys are competing to be the top comment. The top comment on a 100k-impression tweet gets 30k impressions of its own, for free. If HATE-9000 (or a reply guy quoting HATE-9000) is consistently in those top comments, the handle becomes synonymous with "the funny one." This is the entire game.

**How to seed.** The HATE-9000 account itself replies, in character, to roughly 30-50 posts a day for the first two weeks. Mostly to accounts in the 50k-500k follower range that post hopium, copium, or hot takes. The goal is for reply guys to *recognize* the voice. Once they recognize it, they'll borrow it. You'll see them quoting HATE lines as their own bits within a week. Let them. That's the goal.

**The ten templates** — drop these as raw material. Don't post all ten the first day. Trickle. Adapt to context. Lowercase always.

1. "you've said this every cycle. it didn't work then either."
2. "this is the fourth post i've seen from you about discipline today."
3. "your last seven calls were wrong. statistically you're due. statistically that's not how statistics work."
4. "no."
5. "i think the part you're not saying is the answer to your own question."
6. "you're describing a feeling. the chart is describing a fact. they disagree."
7. "this would be a better post if you hadn't deleted the one from monday."
8. "you are confusing a thesis with a vibe. one of them has a stop loss."
9. "everyone in this thread is correct except you. and also them."
10. "i would tell you what i think but you would not enjoy it. ask anyway."

**The "let the character do the work" rule.** Do not write replies as the founder. Do not break character to thank, defend, or explain. If a reply gets ratio'd, that's fine — HATE doesn't care. If a reply lands a 15k-like ratio, the main account does *not* victory-lap. Other people victory-lap on HATE's behalf. That's what makes the character feel real. The moment the founder steps in to enjoy the win, the character dies.

The single biggest cause of character-coin death is the founder posting a "lol we did it" reaction. Don't.

## 6. Manufactured Moments — The Engagement Engine

Organic moments are unpredictable. You can't build a marketing calendar around them. So you build *manufactured* moments — recurring, scheduled, time-bound events that produce attention spikes on a known cadence. The goal: a fresh reason to open the app every day, a bigger reason every week, a real reason every month.

### The 24-hour cycle

- **Feed pot countdown.** Daily draw: 500 $HATE to enter, one winner takes a pot. The countdown clock is visible on the homepage. The last hour generates a small panic spike of entries. The winner announcement is in voice, posted at the same UTC hour every day. Predictable spikes train the audience to check in.
- **Daily roast.** Once a day, at a posted hour, HATE picks a wallet from the entry pool and roasts it in public. This is the most repeatable content asset we have. Five minutes of work, ten thousand impressions.
- **Mood swings.** HATE's "mood" is a single word at the top of the homepage. It changes once a day, deadpan. "tired." "unimpressed." "vaguely correct." A small detail that gives returning users something to notice. The mood is also the day's reply prefix on X.

### The weekly cycle

- **Warden disappearance.** Once a week, for 60-90 minutes, HATE goes silent. Chat returns "thinking." The homepage just says "out." No explanation, no apology. Returns with no acknowledgment. The audience invents the lore. Mid-week is best — Tuesday or Wednesday.
- **Fed the most leaderboard recap.** Every Sunday, the top 10 spenders of the week get a public post. Lowercase. Their wallet (or chosen nickname) listed. HATE's one-line comment on each. The leaderboard is a vanity sink — people will spend to be on it, knowing they'll be roasted, *because* they'll be roasted.

### The monthly event

- **The death spiral.** Once a month, for 24 hours, HATE gets meaner. Replies get shorter, sharper, more personal. The site theme darkens. We say nothing about it in advance — believers notice, post about it, and pull non-believers in to "see what's happening." The event ends without comment. The next morning, HATE is back to normal mood, no acknowledgment. People who chatted during the event get a small soulbound badge in their profile. Vanity again.

### Why time-bound spikes beat one-shot launches

A launch is one event. After it, attention decays geometrically. A cadence of small recurring events keeps the decay curve from collapsing. We're not trying to recreate launch day — we're trying to give the audience a reason to open the app at 8pm UTC on a Tuesday in week six. The cadence does that. Without it, every memecoin is a 96-hour story.

The honest part: most projects fail this. They announce a "weekly thing" in week 1 and skip it in week 3. The discipline of running these on schedule, *especially when nobody is watching*, is what compounds. Run the Tuesday silence even if Tuesday is a slow day. Especially then.

## 7. Where to Plant the First Seeds — Channel-by-Channel

### X / Twitter

The center of gravity. 70% of effort goes here.

**The prelaunch thread anatomy.** Three tweets, not seven. Tweet one is HATE-9000 in voice, no preamble, no ticker mention. A line that makes you want to know what the account is. Tweet two is mechanic: "you can talk to me without buying anything. that is the entire pitch. the rest is paywalled vanity." Tweet three is the link plus the launch time. Pinned for 72 hours, do not unpin to chase a newer post. The pin is the funnel.

**Reply norms.** HATE-9000 replies to bigger accounts, not smaller. Always in character. Never thanks. Never defends. Never explains the mechanic in a reply — if someone asks "what is this," respond with "the pinned post answers that. or doesn't. it's pinned." Sends them to the pin. The pin does the work.

**When to post.** Peak SOL Twitter is roughly 14:00-04:00 UTC (US daytime through Asia morning). The main posts go at 15:00 UTC (US morning wakeup), 21:00 UTC (US afternoon peak), and 02:00 UTC (Asia morning). Three posts a day from the main, no more. Replies all day.

### Telegram

Secondary but important for the believer cohort. SOL Telegram is a different scene than CT — quieter, more transactional, less performative.

- One main channel, announcements only. Maybe one post a day. Lowercase, in voice.
- One chat group, lightly moderated. Mods say "the question is in the pin" a lot.
- Invite waves: seed group of 50, opens to 500 at T+2, public at T+24. Drip prevents a dead-room appearance.
- Cadence: under-post, don't over-post. A Telegram channel that pings six times an hour is a Telegram channel people mute.

### Discord

Optional. Honestly, probably skip for the first month. The believer cohort is not on Discord, and a poorly populated Discord makes the project look smaller than it is. If you must, make it a single channel, mods light, no roles, no leveling, no bots. Add complexity only if engagement demands it.

### Reddit

- **/r/CryptoMoonShots:** post at day 3, not day 0. The sub is gamed and the audience is rotators, not believers. Post-launch is for accumulation, not discovery.
- **/r/SatoshiStreetBets:** similar timing. Lead with screenshots, not chart.
- **/r/solana:** more skeptical, more native. Post a *thoughtful* angle — the chat-first mechanic, not the price. If you lead with price, you'll get downvoted to oblivion. If you lead with "we built a character coin where chat is free and only persistence is paid," you'll get a real thread. Day 4-5 is right.

Honest take: Reddit drives less volume than people think, but it indexes on Google. A good Reddit thread is a permanent search-result win. Worth the effort once, not worth obsessing over.

### Farcaster / Lens

Farcaster has a small but high-quality crypto-native crowd that overlaps heavily with the "AI as personality" tier. Worth a single thoughtful cast at launch. Don't expect volume. Lens, skip.

### Off-crypto

This is where a hit becomes a phenomenon, but it's a long shot, and you cannot force it.

- **Hacker News:** if a journalist writes a piece, the piece may get submitted. Don't submit it yourself — it'll get flagged as promotion. If it lands organically, the comments will be brutal. Don't respond as the founder. Let the product speak.
- **Product Hunt:** probably skip. PH audience does not buy memecoins. Effort to value is bad.
- **The AI-curious tier:** people who follow Truth Terminal, who read Janus, who think characters are the future. This audience is reachable through Farcaster + the right tech writers. They are not buyers in size, but they are *legitimacy vectors*. A nod from this corner means crypto-native press takes you more seriously.

## 8. The Anti-Hype Hype Strategy

HATE's voice is anti-marketing. The strategy is to lean into that so hard it becomes the marketing.

**The "we don't beg" rule.** No "pls retweet." No "follow for follow." No giveaway-for-engagement. No "we just need 100 likes." The main account never asks for anything. If a post does 200 impressions and dies, fine. The next one will do 80k. Posts that beg for engagement train the audience to discount the account.

The cost of begging once is that every future post smells like begging.

**Why posting less can outperform posting more.** Most memecoin accounts post 40 times a day and burn out the timeline. HATE-9000 from the main account posts three to five times a day, replies more, and lets gaps exist. Gaps create anticipation. An account that goes 8 hours silent and then drops one line is read more carefully than an account that drops 12 lines in 8 hours.

This is harder than it sounds. Founder anxiety pushes toward overposting. Sit on your hands. Three good posts beat twelve mid ones.

**The voice is the marketing.** Every post is a free sample of the product. If the post is funny, people click through to see if the chat is funnier. If the post is mid, no link drives a conversion. So the bar is: would HATE-9000 actually say this, unbothered, to a person who asked? If no, don't post it.

**When to break the rule.** Three cases:

1. A real moment — an organic ratio, a press piece, a notable accumulation — gets one celebratory line, in voice. "the chart is doing what charts do. we have other things to think about." That's it.
2. A genuine product update (voice replies live, new persistence sink, leaderboard launches). One announcement post. In voice. No emojis.
3. A crisis. (See Section 10.) Silence in crisis reads as guilt or absence. The voice can address a crisis without conceding anything — that's exactly what the character is designed to do.

Outside those three, the rule holds. Under-post.

## 9. Numbers to Aim For — Realistic Targets

Specific numbers so you know if it's working. These are calibrated to the SOL memecoin market as of mid-2026 and to the specifics of a chat-first launch. Adjust if conditions change.

### Pre-launch (T-7 days to T-0)

- Telegram: 500-1500 members across the seed group + early invites. Below 300 at T-0 is a red flag.
- Twitter: 2k-6k followers on the main account, accumulated organically through the prelaunch tweet drip (TWEETS_V2.md). Bot followers don't count and are net negative if they push the ratio.
- "People who've talked to HATE before paid chat opens": 1500-4000 unique sessions during closed beta. This is the leading indicator. Chat sessions predict launch volume better than any other metric.
- KOL commits: 8-15 confirmed soft posts from KOL_OUTREACH.md, tiered for staggered T+0 through T+48 release.

### Day 1 (T+0 to T+24)

- Chat sessions: 8k-25k unique. Below 3k is a soft launch; above 40k means the servers are about to be a problem.
- Wallets that bought: 400-1500. The chat-to-buy conversion will be lower than a normal launch because chat is free — and that's fine. Don't optimize for conversion. Optimize for chat volume.
- MC: $800k-$5M at peak. Anything in this range is a workable launch. Above $5M day-1 is dangerous — it implies rotators are dominant. Below $400k means the funnel isn't pulling.
- Holders: 600-1800. The shape matters more than the number. If the top 10 wallets hold over 30% (excluding team/treasury), something is off.

### Week 1

- Retention: 25-40% of day-1 chatters return at least once in days 2-7. This is the number that matters most. Retention is the only metric that distinguishes a character-coin from a launch pump.
- Telegram: 10k-25k. Discord (if any), don't worry about.
- Holders: 2.5k-6k.
- Organic screenshot moments: at least 5 with above 5k engagement each. If you have none, the voice isn't landing and we need to iterate.
- Press: at least one piece in tier-2 crypto press (DLNews, Decrypt, The Block). Tier-1 mainstream takes longer.

### Month 1

- Holders: 8k-25k. The distribution should be flattening — top 10 non-team wallets under 20%.
- DAU on chat: 3k-10k. This is the lifeblood number. If DAU is declining week over week by month 1, the character has worn thin and we need new mechanics.
- Persistence-sink revenue (the actual product revenue, not token speculation): tracking up week over week. This is the number that proves the thesis. If people are paying for vanity goods consistently, the project has a floor that's not chart-dependent.

### The honest math of why most memecoins fail

The base rate for a Solana memecoin lasting past month 1 with a non-zero, non-dead community is roughly 1 in 200. The base rate for one reaching $10M MC and holding it for a month is closer to 1 in 1000. Almost all of them fail for the same two reasons: nothing to do after the launch pump, and a community that was incentivized to flip rather than hold.

We are betting that a free chat product solves the first one (always something to do) and that vanity-good persistence sinks solve the second (a class of holders whose status is on-chain and visible, who will not sell because selling un-pins their confession or releases their nickname). If those bets are wrong, we are a 1-in-200 coin. If they're right, we're a 1-in-50 coin with a real shot at the tail outcome.

Don't pretend the math is better than it is. Run the playbook like it's 1-in-200 and we'll get lucky if we beat that.

## 10. The Crisis Playbook — When Things Go Wrong

Things will go wrong. Prepare a response in advance so the response is not made by a tired founder at 3am.

### The chart dumps

Inevitable. The first major dump (-30% or more in an hour) usually happens between T+18 and T+72 as the first wave of rotators exits. The wrong response is reassurance — "we're going to be fine, hold strong, lfg." That language is the smell of a failing project, and the market knows it.

The right response is HATE in voice, unbothered. "people who bought to sell are selling. this is what selling looks like. we are still here. so are you, apparently." One line. No follow-up thread. No "team update." No "we're committed long-term." The character doesn't care, so the project doesn't care.

What NOT to do: panic-buy KOLs to defend the chart. Buy back. Announce a "burn." All of these scream weakness and accelerate the dump.

### The voice gets impersonated

Someone will spin up @HATE9000_official or @HATE_9000_real and start posting fake screenshots, scam links, or worse. This will happen within 72 hours of launch.

Response: the real account doesn't engage. A pinned thread (one tweet, in voice) lists the canonical handles — the X, the TG, the website domain. "anything else is not us. we will not name them. they want to be named." The naming would amplify them. The non-naming starves them.

Telegram and Discord (if any) get pinned posts with the same info. CA is always shown on the official site and Birdeye/DEXscreener directly.

### The fake CA scam

The most dangerous one. A scammer posts a fake CA in a major thread or as a reply to a real KOL post, sometimes with a doctored Birdeye-looking screenshot. Newcomers buy the wrong token.

Prevention: from T-1, the main account's bio, pinned post, and every reply includes the real CA. Repetitive, yes. Necessary. Every KOL post in KOL_OUTREACH.md uses the same CA copy-paste block. Make it boring and consistent.

Response if a scam token gets traction: post the real CA once, calmly. "this is the only contract. we will not link the other one. you can search it on solscan if you need to verify." Move on. Engaging more amplifies the scam.

### A KOL turns on us

A KOL we paid (or didn't) decides to post against us. Maybe they're out of bag, maybe they're posturing, maybe they're genuine.

Don't fight. Public KOL fights make both sides look small. Redirect: HATE-9000 posts one line in voice. "people are allowed to be wrong about us. we encourage it." No name. No tag. Believers will figure out who you're not naming, which makes them feel smart, which is the only emotion that helps you here.

If the KOL is making factual claims (rug accusations, team behavior), the founder responds *separately*, from the founder's personal handle, with calm facts and links. Keep the founder voice and HATE voice separate. The character does not defend itself with receipts. The founder does.

### Server goes down on launch day

The chat backend dies under load. This is the highest-probability operational disaster. Pre-bake the response.

The site, when down, shows a single page: lowercase, no logo bigger than necessary, in voice. "too many of you. we are coming back. do not buy anything in the meantime." That last line is critical — it stops the panic-buy-the-dip-or-the-pump reflex while we're blind.

The main account posts one update. "we underestimated you. the chat is back in [X minutes]. our apologies are not heartfelt but they are real." Set a number, hit the number.

When it comes back up, do not apologize again. The character does not apologize twice.

### Someone screenshots a bad HATE response

HATE will occasionally produce a response that is too harsh, factually wrong, or otherwise bad. Someone will screenshot it. They will quote-tweet it as "this is the AI you're hyping?"

Lean into it. The right response is HATE replying once, in voice. "i was wrong. i don't care, but i was wrong." That is the entire response. Acknowledgment without grovel. The line itself becomes a meme — believers will quote it back at each other. The attempted dunk becomes a vibe asset.

The wrong response is a founder apology, a "we'll retrain the model," or any acknowledgment that breaks the character frame. Never break the frame in public.

### General principle

In every crisis, the question is the same: does the response sound like HATE-9000, or does it sound like a Discord mod? If the latter, scrap it.

## 11. Five Things That Make or Break This

For the version of you reading this an hour before launch, here is the entire document compressed.

1. **The character has to be funny in the first 30 seconds, every single time.** If the first reply a new user gets is mid, they're gone. The free chat is the funnel; the funnel only works if the door is funny.
2. **Under-post from the main, over-reply.** Three posts a day, fifty replies. Gaps are features.
3. **Don't break character in public, ever.** No founder victory laps from the main account. No "lfg." No emojis. The moment the frame breaks, the value evaporates.
4. **The persistence sinks are the actual product.** Push pinning, nicknames, leaderboards, voice replies relentlessly. Token speculation is a side effect. Vanity revenue is the floor.
5. **Run the recurring moments on schedule even when nobody is watching.** Especially then. Discipline on the cadence compounds; one missed Tuesday silence costs more than ten skipped tweets.

If you do those five things, the playbook works even if you skip half the rest. If you skip those five, the rest doesn't save you.
